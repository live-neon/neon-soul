## Brand Authenticity Check

### What Feels Right

1. **Dark neon aesthetic with electric cyan/deep purple** - This visual language conveys technical credibility while remaining warm. The "neon glow effects" (line 251) on headings and CTAs will create visual anchors without overwhelming.

2. **CJK accents as subtle, not primary** - Correct calibration (lines 44-49). Using kanji for atmosphere rather than navigation respects the multicultural team identity without creating barriers.

3. **2-layer approach over audience segmentation** - This respects visitor intelligence. Rather than sorting people into buckets, it lets them find their own depth. This is very NEON-SOUL.

4. **Footer as signature space** - Placing the core equations in the footer (lines 220-229) creates arrival rather than departure. The footer becomes the destination, not the exit.

### What Needs Attention

1. **Tension between boldness and groundedness**: The plan describes "bold, visionary" tone (line 186) for the hero but the project philosophy emphasizes slowness, care, flow. How do these coexist? Suggestion: Bold in vision, grounded in voice. Not shouting, but certain.

2. **The 10-second test may be wrong metric**: For a project about grounding and placing identity, measuring success by speed may import the wrong values. Consider: "Does the surface layer create the right *feeling*?" rather than "Does it communicate in 10 seconds?"

3. **Emoji equations without context may confuse**: The signatures (lines 37-42) are meaningful to the team but may read as decorative to newcomers. Consider: A single sentence of context near the footer signature. Not an explanation, but an invitation: "What does this mean? That's a good question to sit with."

## The Alternative Framing

> Does the plan capture the soul of NEON-SOUL?

Yes, the **architecture** captures the soul. The philosophy is present in the structure - 2-layer depth, grounding journey narrative, signature placement, kotodama framing.

What's missing is the **embodiment**. The plan describes WHAT the page should contain but doesn't yet model HOW it should feel. This is appropriate for a plan (plans describe intent, not experience), but the implementation will need to make the kotodama principle real - every word chosen, nothing wasted, everything alive.

**The central question for implementation**: Can visitors sense kotodama before they read about it?

If yes, the page succeeds. If no, it becomes marketing collateral with Japanese aesthetics.

## Does the Brand Identity Feel Authentic?

**Yes, with one caution.**

The brand identity section (lines 25-68) is comprehensive and philosophically grounded. The visual identity flows from meaning, not decoration. The signatures are integrated, not appended.

**Caution**: The plan uses language like "technical credibility + bold vision" (line 28) which could drift toward tech startup conventions. NEON-SOUL is not trying to "disrupt" anything. It's trying to *place* something. The implementation should resist the gravitational pull of generic tech marketing voice.

**Authenticity test**: If the landing page could belong to any AI project by changing the logo, it's not NEON-SOUL. If it could only be NEON-SOUL, it's right.

## Recommendations

### For Implementation Phase

1. **Write the hero copy first, then test it against kotodama**: Does every word earn its place? If not, remove it. The 10-15 word constraint may be wrong - kotodama cares about necessity, not count.

2. **Map the emotional journey explicitly**: Hero (curiosity/recognition) -> What (understanding/relief) -> How (confidence) -> Start (agency) -> Footer (belonging). Each section should deliver a feeling, not just information.

3. **Choose the longer tagline**: "Identity that knows where it came from" aligns with the grounding journey narrative. It's warmer, more narrative, more NEON-SOUL. "Your AI, grounded" is accurate but could belong to any AI tool.

4. **Add a moment of rest before the footer**: A visual breath between the "Start" section and the footer signature. Let visitors arrive at the equations, not scan past them.

5. **Test with one outsider**: Before finalizing, show the page to someone who knows nothing about NEON-SOUL. Ask: "How do you feel after reading this?" If they can't articulate it but want to keep looking, it's working.

### For the Plan Document

1. **Add an "Emotional Arc" row to the Sections table** (lines 147-157): Surface and Depth layers describe content; add feeling targets.

2. **Resolve tagline in the Resolved Questions table**: Move from implicit decision to explicit choice with reasoning.

3. **Add a "Tone Consistency" acceptance criterion**: Something like "Each section reviewed for tonal alignment with grounding philosophy".
